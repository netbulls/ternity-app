# Session 2026-03-07-6cbd

## Context

Continuation of PDF page-fill optimization. Prior sessions (e9a7, 9f3d, etc.) established continuous flow, sticky headers, and fractional line estimates. This session focused on fixing overflow issues where pages had too many rows, causing Chromium to create extra physical pages.

## Problem Found

After applying "measured" PAGE_LINES budgets based on CSS height calculations, 3 of 7 templates had pages that **overflowed past 297mm**, causing Chromium to insert extra physical pages. Symptoms: physical page count > logical page count, missing footers on overflowed pages, spillover pages with just 1-2 entries.

Root cause: the measured budgets assumed all entries are single-line. In practice, many descriptions wrap to 2-3 visual lines (especially in templates with narrow description columns). The `estimateLines()` function returns fractional values (1, 1.5, 2) but these slightly underestimate actual rendered height for wrapped text. The cumulative error across 30-50 rows per page pushes content past the 297mm boundary.

## Fixes Applied

### V3 (Minimal Swiss)

- PAGE_LINES: 46 → 35
- Was producing 12 physical / 7 logical pages (5 overflow)
- Now produces 9 pages, no overflow

### V4 (Magazine Spread)

- PAGE_LINES: 39 → 30
- Was producing 16 physical / 9 logical pages (7 overflow!)
- Now produces 12 pages, no overflow
- Note: V4 has DESC_CHARS_PER_LINE=55 (narrowest), causing the most wrapping

### V6 (Invoice Style)

- CONT_ROWS: 50 → 44
- Was producing 9 physical / 8 logical pages (1 overflow)
- Now produces 6 pages, no overflow

### Templates unchanged (already correct)

- V1 (Classic Corporate): PAGE_LINES=33, 10 pages
- V2 (Dark Executive): PAGE_LINES=33, 10 pages
- V5 (Dashboard Print): PAGE_LINES=36, 10 pages
- V7 (Cover Chapters): V7_PAGE_LINES=34, 13 pages

## Key Insight

The "measured" CSS height approach gives the theoretical maximum rows, but real-world content with multi-line descriptions needs significant safety margin. The effective budget should be ~65-75% of the theoretical max for templates with narrow description columns (V3, V4) and ~85-90% for wider columns (V1, V2, V5, V7). V6 is different because it doesn't use fractional line estimates (all rows cost 1), but user-header rows are taller than regular rows, which the flat budget doesn't account for.
